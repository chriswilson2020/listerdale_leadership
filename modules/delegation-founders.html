Founder Leadership Development

Based on executive coaching and scaling best practices

Delegation is the single most important skill for founders to master as they scale. Your ability to let go determines whether your company can grow beyond what you personally can do.

Why Founders Struggle to Delegate

Founders often built their companies by doing everything themselves. That scrappy, hands-on approach is essential early on‚Äîbut it becomes a liability as the company grows.

  • "No one can do it as well as me" - Maybe true initially, but your job is to develop people who can
  • "It's faster to do it myself" - Short-term yes, long-term no. You're creating a bottleneck
  • "I don't want to burden my team" - Your team wants ownership and growth opportunities
  • "What if they fail?" - Failure is how people learn. Support them through it
  • "I'll lose control" - You gain leverage. Control through outcomes, not activities

The 70% Rule

If someone can do the task 70% as well as you, delegate it.

They'll get to 90%+ with practice. Meanwhile, you're freed to focus on what only you can do.

The 6-Step Delegation Framework

1

Prepare

Define the task, desired outcome, and resources needed.

2

Assign

Select the right person, communicate clearly, provide authority.

3

Confirm

Verify understanding, answer questions, agree on checkpoints.

4

Monitor

Check in regularly, offer support, provide feedback (avoid micromanaging).

5

Evaluate

Review results, recognize success, learn from failures.

6

Iterate

Refine the process, expand delegation, build capability.

Levels of Delegation

Not all delegation is the same. Match the level to the person's experience and the task's risk:

Level 1
Wait to be told - "Do exactly what I say"
Level 2
Ask - "Look into this and tell me what you think"
Level 3
Recommend - "Give me your recommendation; I'll approve"
Level 4
Act and inform - "Decide and act, then let me know"
Level 5
Act independently - "Handle it; no need to report back"

Goal: Progressively move people up the levels as they demonstrate capability.

Common Delegation Mistakes

  • Dumping, not delegating: Throwing tasks over the wall without context or support
  • Delegating only grunt work: Keep the interesting stuff, give away the boring stuff
  • Micromanaging: Delegating the task but not the authority to complete it
  • Unclear expectations: Not defining what success looks like
  • No follow-up: Delegating and forgetting until it's due
  • Taking it back: Swooping in to "fix" things at the first sign of struggle

Key Takeaway

Delegate to elevate. Let go to grow. Your job as a founder evolves from doing the work to enabling others to do the work. The more effectively you delegate, the more your company can accomplish‚Äîand the more your team can develop.
